## Thesis

Enterprise data-storage company (fka Pure Storage / PSTG) that sells all-flash arrays (FlashArray, FlashBlade) and the Portworx Kubernetes data-services platform. Differentiates via its Evergreen subscription model, converting hardware refresh cycles into recurring storage-as-a-service revenue. Speculative AI data infrastructure play: GPU-intensive AI/ML training and inference workloads generate massive data throughput requirements that favor high-performance all-flash over legacy disk — FlashBlade//S and //E target these workloads directly. Subscription ARR is ~$1.6B as of Q4 FY2025, growing ~24% YoY (per FY2025 earnings call).

## Bull case

- Subscription ARR ~$1.6B, +24% YoY (FY2025 earnings call). Evergreen//One (storage-as-a-service) is the fastest-growing component, shifting capex buyers to opex recurring contracts.
- AI/ML data pipeline tailwind. FlashBlade adopted for AI training data lakes, inference pipelines, and unstructured data workloads. Meta is a disclosed FlashBlade customer for AI infrastructure (per IR disclosures).
- Proprietary DirectFlash Module (DFM) eliminates the SSD controller layer — higher density, lower latency, better endurance vs. commodity SSD competitors (per 10-K). Hardware-level moat that Dell EMC / NetApp do not replicate.
- Non-GAAP operating margin expanded to ~18–19% in FY2025 (per earnings release), driven by subscription mix shift and software-rich portfolio.
- Net revenue retention reported above 120% historically (per earnings calls). Purity OS runs across all Pure hardware, reducing switching costs.

## Bear case & risks

- Hyperscaler concentration risk — a meaningful portion of large deals tied to a small number of accounts. Loss of a top-5 customer (e.g., Meta) is material (per 10-K).
- NAND pricing cyclicality compresses product gross margins in upcycles (as in 2022–2023). No full hedge on NAND cost (per 10-K risk factors).
- Competition from Dell EMC PowerStore, NetApp AFF, and cloud-native storage (AWS EBS/S3, Azure Managed Disks) that disintermediate on-prem/hybrid plays.
- Valuation premium — trades at ~35–45x forward P/E (unverified), pricing in AI acceleration that may not materialize at the expected pace.
- Subscription transition execution risk — shift from product to subscription depresses near-term recognized revenue as contracts spread over time (per 10-K revenue recognition discussion).
