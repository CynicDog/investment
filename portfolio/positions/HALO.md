## Thesis

Best risk/reward in the book and the cheapest of the lot. Halozyme licenses ENHANZE — a recombinant human hyaluronidase (rHuPH20) — to large pharma partners who use it to convert intravenous biologics into subcutaneous formulations. The franchise is a real moat: long-dated, enzyme-based delivery technology embedded in blockbuster drugs (Darzalex SC, others) with high switching costs and royalty economics. Cash-generative, capital-light, with near-term royalty growth from existing approved products and option value from partner pipelines.

## Bull case

- Royalty model = high incremental margins, very capital-light.
- Embedded in a growing roster of approved blockbusters with patent runway.
- Partner pipeline provides multiple shots-on-goal at no R&D cost to HALO.
- Aggressive buybacks at what looks like cheap multiples.

## Bear case & risks

- IP cliff / generic biosimilar dynamics on partner products eventually compress royalties.
- Concentration in a few key partner products near term.
- Biotech sentiment overhangs the multiple even when fundamentals are fine.
- A failed partner Phase III could remove a future royalty stream.
