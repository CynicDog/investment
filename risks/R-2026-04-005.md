## Description

Effective April 6, 2026, the US Section 232 tariff regime was expanded to apply on full customs value (vs. declared metal content only) for articles substantially made of aluminum, steel, or copper; grid and electrical equipment subject to a 15% transitional rate through Dec 2027. ETN's Electrical Americas segment manufactures aluminum/copper-intensive switchgear and transformers. Combined with 130 bps of capacity-ramp margin headwind already embedded in 2026 guidance, layered tariff cost inflation could pressure the $13.00–$13.50 EPS range announced in February. Prior recovery (dollar-for-dollar via pricing in 2025) demonstrated management capability, but the April 2026 re-escalation is incremental and first appears in Q1 2026 results.
