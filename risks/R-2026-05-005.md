## Description

CEO Charles Giancarlo disclosed on 2026-04-23 that NAND/DRAM/CPU input costs surged 300–900% since mid-2025, driven by AI chip demand competing for fab capacity. Everpure raised product prices ~70% and is operating at the low end of gross margin ranges; management chose to partially absorb rather than fully pass through cost increases. Giancarlo stated the supply-demand imbalance will last 'far longer than the COVID-era disruption,' implying a multi-year headwind. Q1 FY2027 results (2026-05-27) are the first reported data point post-disclosure. (Source: Blocks & Files, 2026-04-23: https://www.blocksandfiles.com/flash/2026/04/23/everpure-reveals-supply-chain-issues-warns-customers/5218662)

## Monitor for

Q1 FY2027 earnings gross margin and operating income (2026-05-27); Q2 FY2027 guidance commentary on cost trajectory; Micron/SK Hynix NAND pricing commentary on quarterly calls; any management disclosure of pricing ceiling or cost pass-through timeline.
