## Description

Jerome Powell's term as Federal Reserve Chair expired on 2026-05-15. Kevin Warsh, nominated by President Trump in January 2026, is his designated successor (Senate confirmation status unverified as of 2026-05-15). Warsh served on the Fed Board 2006-2011 and is known for hawkish views on inflation, favoring faster normalization. The transition coincides with headline CPI at +3.8% YoY (April 2026, released 2026-05-12, above consensus) and 10-year Treasury yield at ~4.58%. A new Fed chair with a different reaction function during a structurally inflationary period (Iran/Hormuz energy shock; FOMC April 29 statement cited Middle East uncertainty) could extend higher-for-longer rates or trigger unexpected policy pivots, compressing equity multiples across the portfolio.
